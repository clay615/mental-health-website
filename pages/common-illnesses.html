General information of each illness, their origins, and their most affected demographics are covered for each illness.

Origins

    Though not every person has the same story and past surrounding their mental illnesses, we can look at the most common factors associated with each illness and try to establish the most common reason for their development.

    There are several reasons that mental illnesses may develop. Some of these are environmental factors the person had grown up with or currently lives with. There are also genetic factors. Some illnesses are passed down from parents. There are also chemical reasons, such as unnatural imbalances in the brain.

Demographics

    Another interesting part of mental illnesses is that they effect demographics. This is due to the factors mentioned in the "Origins" section. Some illnesses are more prevalant in women than men. Others are more so in men than women. Gender, race, and background are all factors that can contribute to mental illness.

    Though they can be factors, they arent the sole reason. If someone does not fit the usual demographics for a mental illness, that does not mean they do not have that illness. Demographics are simply pattterns that are observed, not a rule book.
