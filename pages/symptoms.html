Categories of Mental Health Issues

    There are several mental health illnesses that go unnoticed due to our lack of awareness surrounding their symptoms. This can be dangerous for some illnesses such as schizophrenia. For others, such as depressive disorders, this can result in people harming themselves.

Psychotic Disorders

    Psychotic Disorders, such as schizophrenia, are disorders where the person loses their touch with reality, often seeing figures and hearing voices.

Depressive Disorders

    Depressive Disorders occur when there is a chemical imbalance in the brain that makes it difficult for the brain to produce equal hormones, causing a constant emptiness or sadness.

Anxiety Disorders

    Anxiety Disorders are characterized as a constant stress. This can come in many forms. Social anxiety, a common disorder, causes stress when put in socials. OCD on the other hand is an irrational need for certain rituals with the fear of reprecussions if ignored.

Clarification

    Note! This is a guide and is to be used as such. Do not use this website or these articles to diagnose yourself.

    If you believe you have one of these disorders, book an appointment with a psychologist and then receive therapy. Our website is to educate, not diagnose, nor treat.

    If you are currently in a crisis, use our hotlines and contact an expert now! You are needed!

Symptoms of the Disorders

Anxiety

Worrying: Signs of worry can include shaking, paranoia, rapid heart rate, and shortness of breath.

Restlessness: An inability or lack of sleep can be a sign a person is dealing with anxiety that keeps them up at night.

Irritability: Due to constantly being on edge, a person with anxiety may be angry and emotional for no apparent reason, or over the littlest things.

Nausea: Anxiety can cause someone to feel as if they are going to be sick, or even just a constant ache in their stomach.

Psychotic

Hallucinations: Auditory and visual hallucinations are important signs a person may be suffering from a pyschotic disorder.

Delusions: A sign of this disorder type is an inability to distinguish importance from normal occurances. This can cause a person to believe typical things mean something more than they do.

Confusion: This disorder type can result in a lack of memories, altered memories, and a distortion of memories. As such, confusion is common when these memories don't make sense or are challenged.

Emotional Distance: People experiencing a disorder of this type might distance themselves from friends and family. This can be due to paranoia and distrust.

Depressive

Lack of Attention: A short attention span, a lack of desire to understand, and an overall dejected mood all show lack of attention.

Sleep Changes: A lack of sleep or an influx of sleep are both signs someone may be struggling with a depressive disorder.

Emotional Changes: Due to the chemical imbalance, a person suffering from this may have contant mood swings, making them a little unpredictable on how they are feeling.

Harmful Thoughts: Thoughts of harming themselves or others may be an important sign someone is struggling. This could include self harm and suicide.
