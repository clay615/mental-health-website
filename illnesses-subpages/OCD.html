OCD(Obsessive-Compulsive Disorder)

       OCD is an often mischaracterized disorder. Often potrayed as being a disorder surrounding tidiness, it is in reality much different. OCD is a condition that causes a person to do irrational things, that may in the moment seem sudden, with fear of some kind of negative reaction if they fail to do so. This is typically followed by intrusive thoughts. An example would be someone getting the irrational need to wash their hands 10 times before touching a bowl, not because they are a germaphobe, but out of fear of a bad situation, such as death or harm to themselves or loved one, occuring.

What Causes OCD

       Neurological trauma and genetics can cause a person to develop OCD. It can be worsened, or triggered by environmental triggers, such as trauma, stress, or childhood events. There are ranges to OCD, such as mild, moderate, and severe. According to the National Institue of Mental Health (NIMH), of the adults who suffer from OCD, only 34.8% had moderate, and 14.6% with mild. Over half (50.6%) of these adults suffered from serious impairment. The obsessive aspect of the disorder is these intrusive thoughts that stress the person out. The person then compulsively does something, typically a repeated habit, as a way of releasing stress.

Demographics Affected

       About 2.5% of the population is affected with OCD, with men making up 1%, and women making up the other 1.5%. Women have a higher rate of the disorder than men. It is more commonly developed in childhood for men, and in adolesence for women. Though women are predominantly more effected by the disorder, there is no conclusive difference in the outcome of treatment for male and female patients.
