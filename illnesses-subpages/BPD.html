BPD(Bipolar Disorder)

       Some common symptoms of BPD are elevated mood, reduced amounts, and quality of sleep, drastically reduced, or improved self confidence, racing thoughts, and very heavy impulsivity. BPD can also cause darastic mood shifts. People suffering from BPD often go "manic." This is a short period of time where they disregard logic, eventually feeling a crash as this "high" wears down.







What Causes BPD

       Bipolar Disorder can be caused by childhood neglect, major amounts of stress, drug use, and sleep disruption. BPD can be affected by genetics too, and while this doesn't mean you will have BPD just because your parents do. If your family has a history of BPD there could possibly be an increased chance of you developing BPD. As such, if your family has a history and you feel some of the symptoms, it is important to try and contact a psychologist aboit these issues. BPD can harm yourself and / your relationships with others.

Demographics Affected

       Around 1.6% of people have BPD. Around 4-5% of people will experience BPD at some point during their lives. BPD is most commonly diagnosed in the late teens or early twenties. Women will typically not develop BPD till slightly later in life relative to men. People who suffer from BPD often suffer from other disorders and ailments, including drug addictions and eating disorders.
