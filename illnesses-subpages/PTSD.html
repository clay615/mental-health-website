PTSD(Post Traumatic Stress Disorder)

       PTSD causes the re-living of very traumatic moments. Triggers, such as loud sounds, bright bursts of light, and other sudden events can trigger the PTSD. PTSD can cause those suffering from it to be hypervigilant, blame themself for the traumatic event, not trust others, and emotional outbursts. These outbursts can sometimes turn violent, as the person experiencing it is typically in a fight or flight response. Many people suffering from PTSD report not recognizing their loved ones in these fits.







What Causes PTSD

       PTSD is typically caused by traumatizing memories and brain injuries. Some common causes of PTSD are physical and sexual assualt, horrific scenes, combat or war, and the sudden death of loved ones. Outbursts can be caused by reliving even just a glimpse of the traumatizing memory.

       PTSD can be developed during any stage of life. Some people may experience it while they are young, others while they are much older. According to Erase PTSD now, sexual PTSD is not rare with veterans either, and is the realities of many male and female veterans.

Demographics Affected

       Roughly 3.9% of people have PTSD. One of the msot common causes of PTSD is war. Many people in war will see gruesome sights, get badly injured, or go through another scary event, which can cause PTSD. Another leading cause is PTSD is car accidents. Car accidents can happen suddenly, and can be very dangerous, which is why it can lead to car crash victims having PTSD. It is just as easily triggered, considering how common cars are in everyday life, even if you do not personally use them. PTSD surrounding sexual assualt makes it difficult to develop healthy relationships and may cause the view of relationships to warp.
