MDD(Major Depressive Disorder)

       Common symptoms of MDD are persistent sadness, heavy loss interest, feelings of worthlessness and/or guilt, and hopelessness. MDD is a more severe, and worsened version of the more common illness, despression.

What Causes MDD

       There are many different ways MDD can form, but around 8.3% of adults have had MDD. MDD is partially a genetic illness, but can occur if depression goes untreated, and gets much worse.

Demographics Affected

       16% of females have MDD while 10% of males have MDD. Approximately 21.0 million adults in the U.S. had at least one major depressive episode in 2021, representing 8.3% of all U.S.
